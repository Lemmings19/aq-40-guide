Introduction
This guide is intended to provide a comprehensive description of all the encounters in AQ40. It is important to note that it is based on a rogue’s perspective, but could be helpful for other classes as well since everything will be covered in detail. Given how the raid is scaling more in difficulty compared to what we have completed so far, along with the fact that is extremely vast, I have tried to make this guide as intuitive and correct as possible. Due to the length and depth of this guide, I would highly recommend that you navigate using the number designations under ‘Contents’ and Ctrl + F to find the encounter of interest. I also want to mention that I have never cleared AQ40 before, meaning that some information or strategies that I have included might be incorrect. Feel free to provide suggestions if you see something that can be changed/improved - I’m more than happy make adjustments accordingly. Thank you!


Preparation

Compared to previous content, general equipment and consumables are important due to the increased scaling in difficulty. Nature resistance is required in some encounters, but the value will vary depending on raid composition, the amount of healers as well as strategy used. A general rule of thumb is to acquire 255 NR unbuffed (resistance enchants included), but some pieces can be swapped with DPS alternatives when appropriate. List of Nature Resistance gear

Nature Resistance Potions along with Engineering items (grenades, explosives etc.) are highly encouraged to use throughout the dungeon. Furthermore, there will be items and consumables required in specific encounters. These are listed later in the guide.

0.1 - Anubisath Sentinels & Obsidian Eradicators


Anubisath Sentinel



There are two packs of four Sentinels at the beginning of the instance, before the first boss. Each Anubisath has a normal attack that hits for 600 - 800 damage and one random ability, some more fatal than others that must be responded to correctly. These can be revealed by using Detect Magic. Each time a Sentinel falls, the remainder will gain its ability and heal for 50% of their maximum health. Therefore, it is important to kill them in the appropriate order - from the most difficult to the easiest of the abilities.

Abilities and Attacks

Normal Attack: Deals 600 - 800 physical damage.

The random abilities from easiest to hardest:

Magic Reflection: Reflects a particular school of magic to the caster (Arcane & Fire, Shadow & Frost).

Mending: The Sentinel heals itself for 4752 every 3 seconds. Reduced by Mortal Strike.

Knock Away: Periodically knocks enemies away. Immune to taunt.

Thorns: Each melee attack dealt to the Sentinel returns 475 to 525 Nature damage to the attacker. Resistible.

Thunderclap: Deals ~800 Nature damage. No debuff. Resistible. Should be tanked away from the raid and killed by ranged.

Mortal Strike: Deals ~3200 damage to the target and decreases the value of all heals by 50%. Extra healing will
be required.

Mana Burn: Periodically casts mana burn. This type of Anubisath should be tanked away from the raid group at all times.

Shadow Storm: Periodically casts Shadow Bolt Volleys around the Sentinel. Targets all players outside of melee range (<15 yards) and up to some distance >40 yards. Should be tanked in the midst of the raid group at all times or out of line of sight.

Strategy
It is important to eliminate the easiest of the abilities and combinations first, minimizing the most deadly abilities to affect the raid. As a general rule of thumb, the Sentinels that AoE should be killed last to avoid lethal damage or mana burn of the raid, whereas the ones who knockback and heal should be prioritized. Mortal Strike could put a significant strain on healers’ mana when applied on multiple tanks and should therefore be killed late - however, in the case of multiple AoE sentinels (predominantly Thunderclap and Mana Burn since the Shadow Storm damage can be avoided), it has to be killed early to prevent a raid wipe.

Obsidian Eradicator



There are also three Obsidian Eradicators patrolling the beginning of the instance, meaning that they have to be pulled singly and out of range of the Sentinels. They drain mana from all mana users in range and cast a fatal AoE if their mana is allowed to reach 100%. dealing 6.5K Nature damage on everyone within 30 yards if their mana is allowed to reach 100% (24K)

Abilities and Attacks

Shock Blast: Deals 6.5K AoE Nature damage on everyone within 30 yards if their mana is allowed to reach 100% (24K)

Drain Mana: Drains mana from all mana users in range. Chooses 16 candidates to drain mana from as soon as aggro is acquired.

Strategy
This enemy is relatively easy to kill since it deals low damage on the main tank and only has to be mana burned by priests to prevent Shock Blast from being casted. This battle should take no longer than 10-15 seconds.

1 - The Prophet Skeram



Preparation

Greater Arcane Protection Potion x2 (One pre-popped, one after the first one has been depleted)

Greater Nature Protection Potion x2 (One pre-popped, one if the first one has been depleted by Earth Shock damage.

Abilities and Attacks

Basic Melee: Damage dealing ~700-1000 damage.

True Fulfillment: Mind Controls a target very close to the boss, giving them instant target cast, 300% increased damage, resistances increased by 50 and increased movement speed by 40%. Lasts 20 seconds. The target grows far larger in size. This effect cannot be dispelled, but the targets can be sheeped, feared, stunned, blinded or put to sleep if a druid is shapeshifted, and should be done so promptly.

Arcane Explosion: Casts an AoE Arcane spell, dealing 1000-1500 damage in a large radius when there are more than 4 players in melee range. It can be interrupted and have increased cast time using Curse of Tongues or Mind-Numbing Poison.

Teleport: The Prophet randomly teleports to one of his three designated spots and clears the aggro table. Must not be taunted twice after his aggro as been secured to avoid an immediate second teleport.

Earth Shock: If the target that has Skeram’s aggro is out of melee range, he chain casts once every second Earth Shocks dealing 2500 Nature damage each. Generally lethal unless the target has high NR.

Quartile Split: At 75%, 50%, and 25%, Skeram creates two images that have identical abilities to him, retaining his percentage of life but with considerably less hitpoints compared to the true Prophet. Only one is real. Wipes all debuffs on himself whenever he splits. Each image occupies one of his teleport points.

Strategy


Raid placement on The Prophet Skeram

The bulk of the encounter is at 75%, 50% and 25% respectively when Skeram splits into three copies of himself. Positioning of the raid is imperative during this fight, making sure that there are two tanks/furies at each teleport point to pick up the copies, thus greatly reducing the chances of Earth Shock being cast and making sure that the boss can be tanked even when one of the tanks are mind controlled. In the worst-case scenario that two Skerams teleport to a single spot (does not happen during the initial teleport), the OT needs to taunt the newly arrived copy and bring it back to its designated position. It is also important to note that the

The melees and tanks will be distributed evenly around the three respective teleportation spots, ensuring that they are in line of sight for the healers and interrupting the Arcane Explosions as soon as possible. The mages will be split into two groups, positioned in the middle of the upper stairs on each side to make sure that the mind controlled targets can be crowd controlled with ease. Rogues can also use Blind on them for an instant CC. The remainder of the raid will be at the top of the platform, dealing range damage and healing.

1.1 - Qiraji Brainwashers, Vekniss Guardians & Vekniss Warriors

Qiraji Brainwashers are considered one of the weaker enemies in the entire dungeon, accompanied by two Vekniss Guardians. They can be found right after you have defeated Skeram, by the entrance of the Temple’s Corridors and towards the Bug Family room. You will also encounter some Vekniss Warriors in groups of three patrolling here and in the tunnels on the way to Battleguard Sartura.

Qiraji Brainwasher



Abilities and Attacks

Cause Insanity: Immediately Mind Controls a target, increasing its attack speed by 50% and its movement speed by 80%, causing it to attack its own allies for 10 seconds. Can be crowd controlled.

Mana Burn: Hits nearby enemies with anti-mana bolts, draining 925 mana. Also deals damage for each mana point consumed from a target.

Mind Flay: Deals 550 Shadow damage every 1 second.

Vekniss Guardian



Abilities and Attacks

Impale: Rushes a random target, causing that person to be launched high into the air that can send players to their death upon landing and receives a stackable DoT that deals physical damage every 2 seconds.

Enrage: Enrages, increases physical damage and attack speed.

Strategy
The Qiraji Brainwasher will always be killed first since it Mind Controls targets along with using Mana Burn and Mind Flay. Make sure to promptly crowd control the players that are affected by Cause Insanity. It should die quickly when focused.

The Vekniss Guardians need to be dealt with afterwards. Begin to burn them down one by one, ensuring that they are tanked and the raid stacked against a wall to mitigate the fall damage from Impale. In case a player gets launched into the air, it needs to be thrown a heal while in mid-air or when they land since the fall damage combined with the periodical bleed from Impale can result in death.

You can also encounter Vekniss Guardians in large groups of six, so having six tanks is a must when dealing with these. Follow the strategy described above, making sure that you are killing them one by one while stacking against a wall.

1.2 - Vekniss Warrior & Vekniss Borers



Abilities and Attacks

Summon Vekniss Borer: Spawns Vekniss Borers to fight for them upon death.

Strategy

The Vekniss Warriors have no special abilities and come in groups of three. They are incredibly easy to deal with, but become more difficult upon death when 10 Vekniss Borers spawn. These are smaller elite bugs that hit plate users for about 200-300 damage. It is crucial that they are handled correctly by keeping the mages and warlocks healed at all costs while they AoE the bugs down. Also make sure that the single target damage in the raid is only attacking one Vekniss Warrior at a time, and proceed with the next one as soon as the Vekniss Borers have been dealt with.

2 - Battleguard Sartura



Preparation

Limited Invulnerability Potion: To be used during the Whirlwind state.

The Big One: To be used during Sartura’s vulnerability and as much as possible on the Royal Guards.

Abilities and Attacks

Battleguard Sartura

Berserk: After 10 minutes, regardless of her health, Satura becomes enraged, dealing between 10,000 and 15,000 damage to players and gains 150% increased attack speed. This is an almost certain wipe, and must therefore be killed before the 10 minute mark.

Sundering Cleave: This is similar to a warrior’s Cleave, but also reduces the target’s armor by 1512.

Whirlwind: Immune to stuns during this state and gains high movement speed, but otherwise vulnerable. Detect Magic will show when they are using the ability and when they are not.

Reset Aggro: Sartura and her adds occasionally reset aggro for several seconds and start chasing people randomly for 5 seconds. Afterwards, they will return to the target with highest aggro.

20%

Enrage:Satura becomes enraged, increasing her damage by 124 and her attack speed by 60%.

Satura's Royal Guards

Whirlwind: Virtually identical to Sartura’s Whirlwind ability, however not immune to stuns. The Royal Guards deal 1000-1200 damage on tanks and 2500-3500 on cloth.

Knockback: Sends a player flying a significant distance across the room. Make sure not to stay close to the entrance to the tunnel leading to Fankriss to ensure that no trash is pulled.

Strategy


Raid placement on Battleguard Sartura

Battleguard Sartura is a battle of control. Every aspect of this fight must be managed and controlled perfectly in order to ensure success. Because of the 10 minute hard enrage timer, DPS and survivability is extremely important. The boss and her adds will be positioned according to the figure above. The encounter is divided into two phases - the first one revolves around dealing with the adds, whereas the second one is killing the boss.

In the first phase, Sartura and her adds will first be separated - the main tank holds aggro on Sartura in a location back in the room, whereas the remainder of the tanks and furies set up a taunt rotation on the adds to ensure that they are not targetting random raid members. Furthermore, rogues and paladins will assist the tanks by alternating stuns on the add that is being killed using Kidney Shot and Hammer of Justice. Grenades can also be used for the same purpose, however with less reliability. During this time, the adds need to be burned down with focused DPS, killing one by one. Since they have no aggro list, there is no need to worry about threat issues. In addition, every raid member needs to be aware of the positioning of Satura and her adds. If a bug is approaching you, it is imperative that you start running quickly in one direction, thus reducing the chances of getting hit since it is likely to change direction due to its random pathing.

When the adds have died, the second phase begins. The raid needs to spread out in a circular way to minimize AoE damage taken from Sartura. It is crucial that Detect Magic is applied consistently throughout the fight in order to monitor her Whirlwind. Anyone near Sartura will take massive damage, meaning that the healing strain will become much worse along with melees being required to run away of her range occasionally. She is also vulnerable to stuns while not in her Whirlwind state and rogues/paladins should therefore focus on this throughout the fight. At 20%, Sartura enters her soft enrage state, greatly increasing her damage on tanks and melees. Healers should try to conserve their mana until this point, and melees can use Limited Invulnerability Potion while in danger to optimize DPS.

2.1 - Vekniss Soldiers & Drones

The tunnel between Battleguard Sartura and Fankriss the Unyielding is crawling with Vekniss Drones and Soldiers. It is very reminiscent of the Suppression Room in BWL in the sense that the raid group has to be mobile to make it to the other end unscathed with quick respawn timers (approximately 20 seconds). The Drones have very low health and pulling the Soldiers will aggro the Drones as well.

Vekniss Soldier



Abilities and Attacks

Aggro Drones: Causes the Vekniss Soldiers to draw Drones to assist it.

Cleave: Inflicts 110% of normal melee damage to an enemy and its nearby allies, affecting up to 3 targets.

Debiliating Charge - Charges an enemy, dealing damage and slowing their movement by 60% for 8 seconds.

Frenzy Aura - Increases nearby allies’ attack speed by 20% and attack damage by 20%.

Strategy
All casters should AoE the weaker Drones whereas the melee attackers burn down the more powerful Soldiers. The mobs respawn quickly, so they must be killed on the run. Anyone who dies should ghost run, zone back into the instance and receive a summon before the next boss. At the end of the tunnel is a safe spot in a small alcove, where the raid can be summoned and rebuffed before the next encounter. Note that it is important not to get too close to Fankriss as his aggro radius is large, so try to stay within the safe spot before the group is ready to prevent wipes.

3 - Fankriss the Unyielding



Abilities and Attacks

Basic Melee: Very light damage on the MT

Mortal Wound: Stackable debuff on the MT that reduces healing by 10%. Cleave attack, meaning that it can hit multiple people in front of him. Rotate him between two tanks to allow it to wear off.

Entangle: One player is teleported and rooted in one of three small alcoves around the room with 4 Vekniss Hatchlings (these spawn shortly after the pull and thereafter once every minute). These hit for 300 on tanks, 800-900 on cloth and can be snared or feared. They will need to be off-tanked for most of the fight. If the Vekniss Hatchlings get loose, they need to get killed or kited. It is preferable to kill them in early stages of the fight and kited later on, for example using Frost Trap or fears. They cannot be snared.

Summon Worm: At random times during the fight, one to three yellow-colored level 63 Elite worms called Spawn of Fankriss will spawn periodically in a random location. These hit for around 2000 on cloth and will enrage after 20 seconds, increasing attack and movement speed by 150% and all damage by 500% with taunt immunity. Enraged worms will one-shot anybody they are on, dealing 10-20k per hit. They are extremely important in this encounter as they must be killed immediately. However, since they are vulnerable to stuns, it is possible to salvage the situation even after they enrage.

Strategy
Fankriss is a chaotic battle. It requires a combination of excellent crowd control, split second reactions and good DPS. Fankriss must be tanked in the middle of the room to avoid drawing aggro from the Drones in the tunnel. The battle starts off slowly and becomes more intensive as it progresses.

Two tanks will be on Fankriss due to the application of a healing reduction debuff (Mortal Wound) on whoever is tanking him. It can stack up to ten times for a total of 100%, rendering all heals useless. One will take him until he has about four to five stacks of the debuff and thereafter the second tank will taunt off in order to allow it to wear off. This will continue until Fankriss is dead.

In the meantime, the rest of the raid should be dealing as much damage to Fankriss as possible. However, it is important that the Vekniss Hatchlings are dealt with if they accumulate, using crowd control (predominantly AoE fears) and off-tanks picking them up right after a player has been entangled. Depending on the DPS, these are fairly easy to handle and most of the focus should be put on damaging Fankriss and keeping them away from the casters/healers.

The final and most crucial aspect of this encounter is the Spawns of Fankriss. These Elite worms spawn periodically and attack the raid. This should be a priority for all DPS, and the attention must be shifted to these in order to kill them as quickly as possible. These have 34K HP and enrage after 20 seconds, dealing lethal amounts of damage. They are however vulnerable to stuns and should be crowd controlled as much as possible have they not been killed at this stage.

In summary, it is important that the damage is always strong on Fankriss, that the tanks are transitioned properly along with controlling the Hatchlings and dealing with the Spawns promptly. Also note that the waves of bugs have to be dealt with after Fankriss dies and should be crowd controlled and killed to mitigate deaths.

3.1 - Vekniss Hive Crawlers



Abilities and Attacks

Poison Bolt: Shoots poison at an enemy, dealing 2250 Nature damage and then 500 damage every second for 10 seconds.

Crippling Poison: Slows movement by 50%. Lasts 12 seconds.

Mind-Numbing Poison: Slows casting speed by 30%. Lasts 12 seconds.

Sunder Armor: Reduces armor by 1000 per stack. Can be applied up to 5 times. Lasts 20 seconds.

Strategy

These giant scorpions come in pairs of two right after Fankriss has been defeated. Although they are not overly difficult to deal with due to their low HP pool, it is crucial to cleanse the ranged high damage poisons immediately while keeping the tanks healthy.

3.2 - Vekniss Wasp Packs

These packs are encountered shortly after Frankiss, but before Princess Huhuran. Each group contains two or three Vekniss Wasps, a Vekniss Stinger, and/or a a Qiraji Lasher.

Vekniss Wasp



Abilities and Attacks

Itch: Inflicts an itch on the target. 10 seconds later, it turns into Vekniss Catalyst.

Vekniss Catalyst: Reduces movement speed by 50%. Deals 514 damage every 2 seconds and takes 10x damage from Vekniss Stingers’ charge. Can be dispelled.

Vekniss Stinger



Abilities and Attacks

Charge: Randomly charges a target, dealing a low amount of damage. If Vekniss Catalyst is applied to the target by Vekniss Wasps, the damage from Vekniss Catalyst is multiplied by 10, making it lethal.

Qiraji Lasher



Abilities and Attacks

Whirlwind: Virtually identical to Sartura’s Whirlwind ability, however not immune to stuns.

Knockback: Sends a player flying a significant distance.

Strategy

Right after they have been pulled, it is important for the tank to secure aggro on the Wasps and Stingers at the front of the raid, while a hunter pulls the Lasher to the back of the raid. All melee DPS should begin attacking the Stingers first, while ranged DPS focuses the Lasher. Similar to the Battleguard Sartura encounter, the Lasher requires two tanks since it randomly drops aggro and uses Whirlwind, dealing lethal damage. The mob should be kept stationary as best as possible while it is burned down. It can be stunned, similar to Sartura’s Royal Guards and the Whirlwind can be monitored using Detect Magic.

It is important to be careful about the interaction between the Wasp and Stinger. If Vekniss Catalyst is applied to the target by the Wasps, it can prove to be lethal. There is not much that can be done about the charge, but the poison can be dispelled.

4 - Princess Huhuran



Preparation

Greater Nature Protection Potion x2 (One pre-popped, one at 30%)

~255 Nature Resistance unbuffed

Abilities and Attacks

Frenzy: Huhuran’s damage and attack speed is increased. Can be removed with Tranquilizing Shot.

Poison Bolt: Deals direct 2000 Nature damage to the closest 15 people. Only occurs when she Frenzies or when she Berserks.

Noxious Poison: Deals 2900 Nature damage over 8 seconds and silences the target. Hits a random person and anyone around her. Cannot be cleansed.

Acid Spit: Deals 219 - 281 damage every 2 seconds. Stackable DoT on the primary target only. Not resistible.

Wyvern Sting: AoE sleep effect on a random area within melee range that affects up to 10 targets. Deals 3000 Nature damage if cleansed.

30%

Berserk: Huhuran’s attack speed doubles, and she releases 2000 damage poison bolts every 3 seconds to the closest 15 people. (500 with 315 NR)

Strategy

Princess Huhuran is the first real gear dependent boss in the instance. Every rogue and warrior needs to have NR gear in excess, approximately 255 unbuffed for rogues. At the start of the encounter, two tanks will be at the front with the boss turned away from the raid at all times. A tank switch is necessary occasionally because of her Acid Spit. This is because it stacks infinitely and cannot be resisted, along with the fact that it deals substantial damage after only a few stacks. The debuff lasts for 30 seconds. In addition, hunters need to use Tranquilizing Shot as soon as she goes into Frenzy.

The 15 players closest to Huhuran (predominantly melees and tanks) should have their full NR gear and take up their positions immediately. The casters and remaining players will then move into position behind Huhuran. Depending on the raid composition, e.g. the amount of healers, some NR gear can be swapped out for DPS gear. Wyvern Sting is an important skill to keep track of - it is an AoE sleep effect that will hit the ten closest people. It is extremely imperative not to dispel anyone but the tanks since it causes 3000 damage instantly, however can be resisted. Due to the fact that the boss cannot be taunted, it has to be removed from the tank that is trying to get aggro.

It is a relatively simple fight until she reaches 30%. Therefore, it is possible for rogues to equip regular gear prior to reaching 30%, thereafter using Vanish and changing to NR gear when appropriate. When she reaches this point, she enters a Berserk phase and begins to shoot Poison Bolts onto the closest fifteen players. High Nature Resistance as well as Prayer of Healing is crucial here. This is the phase when all of the cooldowns should be popped, including the second Greater Nature Protection Potion.

4.1 - Anubisath Defenders



Right after Princess Huhuran has been slain, 5 Anubisath Defenders have to be dealt with before moving on to the Twin Emperors. These are considered to be one of the most difficult trash of the whole dungeon, meaning that they must be taken seriously to prevent wipes. They have about 450,000 HP each and will periodically summon two different kinds of adds.

In addition, each Defender comes with a combination of two different abilities that must be reacted to properly, or it brings doom to the raid. These will be determined from 4 sets of ability groups that it can choose from, where 1 out of 2 abilities will be selected from a specific set for a total of 4 abilities. These two abilities are opposite to each other with regards to raid placement - one will favor close range and stacking, while the other will require the raid to spread out.

Abilities and Attacks

Set 1: Meteor or Plague

Meteor: Targetted AoE damage in an 8 yd radius zone, dealing 15,000 Fire damage to players within it. The damage is split between 15 players, meaning everyone affected by the ability will take 1000 damage each and 30 players 500 damage each. FR reduces the amount of damage taken by the spell.

Plague: Infects a random target with Plague, causing it to deal Nature damage to itself and any nearby allies every 3 seconds for 40 seconds. Cannot be cleansed. NR reduces the amount of damage taken by the spell. 5 yd radius. Run away from the raid until the timer expires, while receiving heals the entire duration. The MT is likely be the first target of the Plague, so the whole raid group must avoid him or her.

Set 2: Shadow Storm or Thunderclap

Shadow Storm - Fires an AoE Shadow Bolt Volley in rapid succession, dealing ~1000 Shadow damage. SR reduces the amount of damage taken by the spell. The spell has a dead zone, meaning that the damage can be avoided if standing close to the Defender. Range of 20-45 yds.

Thunderclap - Short ranged Nature AoE damage that emanates around the Anubisath Defender. No debuff. NR reduces the amount of damage taken by the spell. Range of 20 yds.

Set 3: Reflect Arcane & Fire or Reflect Shadow & Frost

Fire and Arcane Reflect: Reflects Arcane and Fire spells back to the user, dealing Arcane or Fire damage. Can be detected using Detect Magic.

Shadow and Frost Reflect: Reflects Shadow and Frost spells back to the user, dealing Shadow or Frost damage. Can be detected using Detect Magic.

Set 4: Enrage or Explode

10%

Enrage: Standard Enrage used near death. Counteract this by adding extra healing for the MT.

Explode: Only used near death, causing the Defender to explode after 5 seconds, inflicting a significant amount of Fire damage. The Anubisath’s hands will glow aflame like a mage casting a Fire spell. It is rooted in place - run away.

Summon Guards :The Defender periodically spawns Anubisath Warriors (small beetle-like creatures with 38,000 HP that can be off-tanked, ice trapped or feared) or Anubisath Swarmguards (6,500 HP with cleaving ability, similar to the appearance of Qiraji Lashers) to attack the raid. The adds cap after four are spawned and they will despawn after 1 minute, regardless of how much HP they have.

Strategy

If a melee DPS gets affected by Plague, it is possible to have the tank and melees far enough apart that they don’t damage the MT.

A good general strategy is to have everyone stand in a tightly packed group. The Defender can be pulled to the group, completely avoiding Shadow Storm. The Thunderclap can be healed through rather easily, especially using PoH. The Meteor will be spread out amongst everyone. It can be burned down before it explodes if DPS is adequate. The only time anyone needs to move out of the group is if they are affected by Plague. Priests/warlocks should set up a fear order to chain fear adds until the Defender is dead.

5 - The Twin Emperors



This is a battle that requires flawless execution to beat them. In their room, Qiraji Scarabs and Qiraji Scorpions are scattered all around the floor, and if killed, they constantly respawn throughout the fight. They are initially neutral, but turn hostile when hit by AoE spells or when mutated by Emperor Vek’nilash

Preparation

Greater Frost Protection Potion x2 (One pre-popped, one after that first one has been depleted)

High Shadow Resistance (Only applies to warlock tanks)

Abilities and Attacks

Heal Brother: If Vek’lor is too close to Vek’nilash (within 60 yds) both of them begin to constantly heal each other for 30,000/tick. They have to be far apart.

Twin Teleport: Every 30-40 seconds, the two twins will trade places with each other, visible by a blue glow around their feet. They will be rooted for 2 seconds after the teleport before starting attacking again. The aggro table will be reset and the Emperors attack the target closest to them, thus allowing that particular player to generate high amounts of threat.

Berserk: At the 15 minute mark, the Emperors will berserk, increasing their movement speed by 150% and damage dealt by 500%. Typically results in a wipe, but it is possible to down them if they have very low HP left.

Combined Health: The Emperors share percentage health, but not their real one. Damage to one Emperor hurts the other emperor as well. Vek’nilash’s HP is about 1.6M, while Vek’lor’s is about 1M. Thus, 100k damage done to Vek’lor equates to 160k damage done to Vek’nilash, allowing them to die simultaneously. This favors casters over melee DPS in this fight.

Emperor Vek'nilash - Uses a sword and immune to all spell damage except Holy.

Uppercut: Vek’nilash randomly turns and attacks someone in his melee range, dealing damage and knocking them away. All melee DPS must be at full health while attacking to avoid getting killed by a second Uppercut on the same target.

Unbalancing Strike: Inflicts 350% weapon damage to a target and reduces their defensive skill by 100 for 6 seconds. It is imperative that most tanks have over 400 defensive skill during this battle since they will receive too much damage to heal through.

Mutate Bug: Increases the size, health and damage dealt of a nearby bug by 300% and 1800% respectively and attacks the raid. This occurs every 10-15 seconds.

Emperor Vek'lor - Caster, immune to all physical damage and has mana.

Shadow Bolt: Vek’lors main attack, dealing 3000-4000 Shadow damage each to a single target. Partially resistible. He casts this at whoever is closest to him at the time. Nobody else apart from the tanks can be in range or his aggro becomes chaotic and unpredictable.

Blizzard: Slows and damages everyone within its area of effect. Deals 1500 Frost damage/tick. Use Greater Frost Protection Potion to mitigate damage whenever possible.

Arcane Burst: AoE ability that deals 4000-4950 Arcane damage whenever a melee is in range. Knocks back anyone it hits and slows their movement speed by 70%.

Explode Bug: Every 7-10 seconds Emperor Vek’lor infuses a nearby bug with energy and causes it to explode after 3 seconds, dealing dealing significant AoE Fire damage in a large radius. The bug’s legs glow red for a few moments before this happens.

Qiraji Scarabs/Qiraji Scorpions

Virulent Poison: Stackable DoT that randomly procs on melee attack.

Pierce Armor: Reduces an enemy’s armor by 50% for 10 seconds.

Bugs are triggered by their corresponding Emperor - Vek’nilash mutates bugs within 25 yds, Vek’lor explodes bugs within 30 yds.

Mutate Bugs: Grow to 400% size, gain 300% health and melee for 1800% damage, as well as turn hostile. They must be tanked/killed since they otherwise stay mutated for 4 minutes and melee for 1000+ damage per hit.

Explode Bugs: Grow to 200% size and their legs start glowing red, exploding 3 seconds after they are affected by the ability and deal 2813-3187 Fire damage.

Strategy


Raid placement along with other valuable information in this fight.

Pre-position the raid as shown on the figure and proceed with the pull. Use a hunter to pull Vek’nilash to 1-2 warrior tank(s) and use 1-2 warlock(s) to secure aggro on Vek’lor. It is important that the whole raid understands and reacts to all of their attacks, most notably moving out of Blizzard, avoiding Arcane Burst by staying away from Vek’lor in addition to the bug dynamic and the Twin Teleport.

This is a long fight, meaning that it cannot be rushed. Perfect execution outweighs any sort of speed and prevents wipes. The majority of the casters should position themselves on the stairs, healing the main tanks and defeating enraged bugs. If Vek’lor unleashes a Blizzard upon the stairs, it is crucial that it is moved out from swiftly or it will most definitely result deaths. Greater Frost Protection Potion is extremely useful during this battle and should be used as much as possible to mitigate the damage.

It is important that no AoE abilities are used at all during this fight since they usually hit the bugs, making the others in the vicinity to aggro. Each of their attacks stacks a deadly poison that gets out of control fast. In the case of the neutral bugs getting pulled, gather them up and kill them quickly with AoE spells before anymore can aggro.

All damage classes have significant roles in this encounter. There is a setup for each of the Emperors and it needs to be executed perfectly, making sure that they are controlled at all times during Twin Teleport.

Vek’nilash: This Emperor will be tanked by warriors, but they need to ensure that they have above 400 defense each and have their backs against a wall to prevent them from flying backwards. Once aggro has been solidified on the MT, the melee DPS will start attacking the boss. When the teleport is closing in, around 5 seconds before, the second warrior tank will head over to the opposite teleport point to pick up the aggro as soon as Vek’nilash appears. The melee DPS will also move to the other side at this point. This will be repeated throughout the whole fight.

Vek’lor: Since this Emperor is immune to physical damage, it will be tanked by warlocks with high Shadow Resistance gear and linked to their Voidwalkers. As soon as Vek’lor appears at the teleport spot, the assigned warlock must begin chain-casting Searing Pain to draw his Shadow Bolt. It is important that nobody is in his melee range to prevent Arcane Burst from being cast.

It is important that the raid group allows both the warrior and warlock tank for each side to cement aggro before the rest of the raid begins attacking after Twin Teleport. In addition, it is important to move away the exploding bugs from the raid as well as promptly killing the mutated bugs to prevent unnecessary deaths.

You will encounter these Qiraji packs immediately after the Twin Emperors. They are arguably the most difficult trash encounter of the dungeon, and should therefore be handled with caution. Each pack consists of one Qiraji Champion and any combination of four Qiraji Slayers and Mindslayers.

Qiraji Slayer

Strategy

The first enemy that needs to be focused is the Mindslayer. These often cast Mind Flay, resulting in significant strain on the healers. It is immune to taunt, so it is important to make sure that the tank has generated enough aggro before burning it down. The players affected must promptly be polymorphed or stunned immediately to prevent deaths. The Mindslayer should be pulled away from the raid before it dies to minimize the fear, damage and mana draining effect from Mana Burn.

Next, the Slayers need to be eliminated. Make sure that they are tanked away from the casters at all costs since they have an AoE silencing spell when under the effect of Frenzy. This also ensures that only the tank is being cleaved. In the meantime, hunters need to pay special attention to the Qiraji Slayers since they enrage often, requiring a Tranquilizing Shot. A hunter should be assigned to each and wary of their positioning. Every fifteen seconds or so the Slayer knocks the tank away, causing the Slayer to attack whoever is second on their aggro list. This effect can be mitigated by tanking it while their back is facing a wall.

The final kill will be the Qiraji Champion. Keep him tanked away from the raid using two tanks with their backs against a wall due to the knockback. Fear Ward needs to be applied on the tank as much as possible. The final ability to watch out for is Vengeance when the Champion reaches low hitpoints. This will cause him to enter an enraged state and should be dispelled immediately to prevent unnecessary damage on the tank.

5.2 - Anubisath Warders & Obsidian Nullifiers



These trash mobs will be encountered at the very end of the instance, right before C’Thun and Ouro. They come in packs of 1 Anubisath Warder and 2 Obsidian Nullifiers and there will be 3 pulls in total. While it may seem a bit daunting at first, this trio is easily dispatched if handled correctly. They follow the same trend as the other Anubisath and Obsidian mobs encountered in AQ - the Anubisath Warder selects a random ability at the beginning of combat. It will choose 1 out of 2 abilities from 2 distinct sets. The Warder will reset its abilities if the raid wipes. The Nullifiers have an ability that is triggered when they reach full mana, and they acquire that from draining it from the raid.

Anubisath Warder

Abilities and Attacks

Set 1: Entangling Roots & Fear

Entangling Roots: Immobilizes a large quantity of the raid for a long duration. Ranges at least 30 yds from the mob and occurs periodically. This is detrimental to the melee classes if they become rooted out of range of anything they can hit.

Fear: Fears a large quantity of the raid for up to 8 seconds. Ranges at least 30 yds from the mob and occurs periodically. Fear Ward should be ready at all times for the warrior tanking the Warder. You obviously do not want your raid to get feared while damaging and draining the Nullifiers since time is of the essence.

Set 2: Dust Cloud & Silence

Dust Cloud: Reduces the chance to hit by 85%. Since these are level 63+ mobs, this essentially stops all melee damage. Make sure melees are not afflicted by this while they are attempting to defeat the Nullifiers.

Fire Nova: Deals significant AoE Fire damage to anyone in the vicinity. It’s a large factor in wiping when used in conjunction with with Obsidian Nullifier’s Nullify.

Obsidian Nullifier

Abilities and Attacks

Cleave: Inflicts 110% of normal melee damage to an enemy and its nearby allies, affecting up to 3 targets.

Nullify: When its mana bar is full, it casts an AoE spell that banishes players and reduce their hitpoints to 1. When used in conjunction with a Warder’s Fire Nova, this can lead to many deaths. Additionally, Nullify removes all buffs from afflicted players, including Soulstones and Ice Block.

Drain Mana: Continually drains mana from the raid group, filling up their own mana bar as a result.

Strategy

The Obsidian Nullifiers should be the first to die and they must be dispatched promptly to prevent them from draining too much mana from the raid along with preventing them from being able to use Nullify. Have everyone in the raid that can drain mana to do so, predominantly priests and warlocks, and split them in half. Half of the raid should be mana draining one target, while the other half drains the other. While this is happening, the tanks should be grabbing their aggro as quickly as possible. All damage should be focused on one Nullifier at a time. If the damage is split over both Obsidians you run out of time before they fill their mana bar.

The Warder is not an overly powerful foe, but does have a few abilities that can make dealing with the Nullifiers more difficult. Ensure that the Warder is tanked far in front of the raid, so that any abilities the Anubisath has does not affect the raid. Turn the focus to it as soon as the two Obsidian Nullifiers are dead.

6 - C'Thun

This encounter is effectively split into two phases - the Eye of C’Thun first and C’Thun afterwards after the Eye has been killed.

Preparation

Greater Nature Protection Potion in excess (Pre-pop one).

Greater Shadow Protection Potion in excess (Pre-pop one).

C'Thun Warner (Addon)

Phase 1 - Eye of C’Thun



The Eye of C’Thun battle is a dance of raid movement. Once mastered, nobody should die. Executing this phase with near perfection is necessary as well, since you want the majority of your raid group alive when CThun rises.

Abilities and Attacks

Eye Beam: For 50 seconds, C’Thun assaults the raid with a green laser beam, dealing 2.6k-3.4k Nature damage every 3 seconds. Resistible. If this hits one person, they almost always survive. Chains between players that are too close to each other (within roughly 10 yards) and indefinitely increases in damage each time it chains until there are no more players within range of the beam. If two players are near one another, one will take the normal amount of damage, and the next takes even more, estimated to be multiplied by a factor of 1.5. Should this hit more than two players it is 100% fatal. The importance of being spaced out during this phase of C’Thun cannot be stressed enough. The safety range is measured at 15 yards - use the C’Thun Warner addon to verify this. Use Greater Nature Protection Potion to mitigate damage taken during this phase.

Dark Glare: Deals 43k-56k Shadow Damage on any player who passes through the large beam, which occurs every 45 seconds, extends radially outward from C’Thun during the ‘red’ phase of stage 1. The eye will appear visibly red and slowly rotate 180 degrees clockwise or counterclockwise The initial target is random, but there are signs of where the glare will begin due to a shadowy cloud appearing in the pupil shortly before the beam is let loose. It is possible to blink through it.

The moment you see the beam charge up, it is time to start moving. If the Eye is facing you when charging you have about 4 seconds to get out of the way. The Eye Beams stop at this point, so spacing is not necessary until the Dark Glare is finished. Take note of where you are standing before the Dark Glare. Your group, when the Glare is over, should be in the opposite position of the room. After the next Dark Glare, your group should be back in its original position etc.

Spawn Claw Tentacle: Tentacles with 1,500 HP will periodically spawn at random locations with alarming frequencies at various points of C’Thun’s chamber, dealing approximately 200 melee damage per hit and do a one-time knockback attack when they are spawned called Ground Rupture for 1,350 - 1,650 damage. Additionally, they can do a Hamstring attack, making it more difficult to flee from the Dark Glare. Can sometimes knock players into the Dark Glare. The player who gets knocked away must get back into position immediately to keep true to the spacing rule, even if they have to get into melee range of the newly spawned claw. All melee DPS should immediately shift over and destroy it before it can burrow underground again.


Spawn Eye Tentacles: Every 45 seconds CThun summons eight Eye Tentacles with 2,300 HP that remain stationary and equally spaced in a circle around him at the black expanse, knocking back anyone standing within a few yards of their spawn points. It is important to stay away from the area until they actually spawn. These eyes have a Mind Flay spell that deals 750 Shadow damage per second for 3 seconds to a random target, but are susceptible to many crowd control abilities such as Counterspell, Gouge, and stuns. A team of one melee and one caster is enough to kill them in 4 seconds. Other ranged may help, but no more melee or they risk chaining the Eye Beam. Mind Flay be resisted, but its damage won’t be reduced. Abilities that remove slowing effects, e.g. Sprint, counteracts it.

Strategy


Illustration of the raid placement and movement on C’Thun.

When first entering C’Thun’s chamber, the best strategy is to shield a player with high NR and have him enter the room ahead of the rest of the raid. This will cause the first few Eye Beams to hit this particular player while giving the rest of the raid a chance to get into position. A hunter is ideal for this due to their +60 NR aura (Aspect of the Wild) as well as ability to FD and equip regular gear. If the raid enters the room at the same time it is likely that a large portion will die from a chained Eye Beam. An alternative is to use a tank with priest shield and Greater Nature Protection Potion.

Once in position, the ranged DPS burn away at the Eye of C’Thun with maximum DPS. The Eye itself as no aggro properties or way to deal damage other than the Eye Beams and Dark Glare. When Claw Tentacles and Eye Tentacles spawn it is the duty of everyone, but the ranged DPS to deal with them.

When the Dark Glare comes, shift the raid clockwise or counterclockwise accordingly. Make sure you are in position a good 5 seconds before the Glare is over. Once over, the Eye Beams begins again in full force. During Dark Glare a few warriors should rush into melee range with the Eye while they move and apply five Sunder Armors, increasing the melee DPS. Repeat these step until the Eye is dead.

Phase 2 - C'Thun



When the Eye of C’Thun dies, C’Thun rises. If no tentacle is alive at this point, players are taken out of combat and are encouraged to drink during this transition. During this phase, C’Thun has a shield that reduces all damage by 99%. In order to bring the shield down and damage it, players must engage the stomach from inside. This is an extremely long battle, and the success rate is determined by how fast the raid can react to the chaos around them.

Several things are similar to the Eye phase, but usually in larger and more powerful quantities. The largest difference is that the spacing rules no longer apply due to Eye Beam not existing during this phase. The body of C’Thun himself has no real abilities to speak of, but there will be many things around you that demand immediate and proper reactions or things can get out of hand quickly.

Abilities and Attacks

Spawn Giant Claw Tentacle: Once every minute, with a 30 second off-set from Giant Eye Tentacle spawns, an enormous version of the Claw Tenticle spawns with 90,000 HP, appearing at a random person’s feet and dealing 3,000 - 4,000 knock away damage through Ground Rupture to anyone caught above it when it surfaces. This must be tanked immediately since it deals 1,500 damage on a well-geared tank and also have a Thrash ability (two extra attacks) that can one-shot someone with low armor. Whichever warrior is closest should engage it within 5 seconds. If left above ground with no target for too long, it heals to full and burrows under, only to spawn again shortly and use the Ground Rupture ability again. Ranged DPS cannot pull aggro on these, so they should attack immediately. Rogues and other melee DPS, however, should wait for aggro to be established. Kill the tentacle as quickly as possible, but do not ignore the other things happening in the chamber. Although they are important to get down, they are actually the lowest priority of things to kill. There appears to be no cap on the number of tentacles that can be spawned at one time.

Spawn Giant Eye Tentacle: Once every minute, with a 30 second off-set from Giant Claw Tentacle spawns, C’Thun will summon a Giant Eye Tentacle with 36,000 HP. They have an identical Ground Rupture ability upon spawning, and also melees just as hard. They repeatedly cast the Eye Beam ability that the Eye of C’Thun uses in Phase 1, but are susceptible to a wide range of crowd control abilities like their small version. There appears to be no cap on the number of tentacles that can be spawned simultaneously. Since the whole raid is packed together to deal with these, players are vulnerable to Eye Beam. It is therefore imperative that they are permanently stunlocked and killed fast.

Spawn Eye Tentacles: As in phase one, C’Thun summons the eight smaller Eye Tentacles around him. However, they spawn every 30 seconds now. Eliminating these is the highest priority as they can result in tremendous damage on the raid. While most of the DPS will be concentrated in one part of the room, taking down Giant Claws and Giant Eyes, the Mages should spread out around the room and be ready to destroy these Tentacles promptly. Some of the DPS must take these down within 5 seconds, or the raid is likely to wipe.

Consume: Every 10 seconds, a tentacle will emerge and swallow a random person in the raid, placing them in C’Thun’s stomach. You fall from the top of the room into green acid, and are given a stacking debuff called Digestive Acid, dealing significant Nature damage. While in the stomach, there are two Flesh Tentacles near two islands with a melee attack and 25,000 HP each that need to be killed in order to make C’Thun vulnerable to attacks. Some classes are better at this than others. If the Flesh Tentacles are not destroyed together, they will respawn after 1 minute.

Digestive Acid: Stackable debuff dealing 150 Nature damage every 3 seconds per stack when inside C’Thun’s stomach. It reapplies itself every 4 seconds and stacks up to 99 times. Cannot be mitigated by resistances. Pay attention to your health and leave before it stacks too greatly. Nobody should die in the stomach. Greater Nature Protection Potions help to increase the time that you can be inside the stomach.

Weakened C'Thun: After killing the Flesh Tentacle’s in C’Thun’s stomach, he turns purple and becomes vulnerable for 45 seconds. This phase needs to be use to burn him down. If there is a Giant Claw alive, don’t stop tanking it. During the weakened phase, C’Thun doesn’t spawn any more tentacles. At the end of the phase, C’Thun regains its shield and the Flesh Tentacles respawn in his stomach along with a Giant Claw Tentacle 10 seconds in his chamber afterwards. If done correctly, 2-3 weakened states are required to kill him.

Strategy
This battle boils down to discipline and reaction. If the Tentacles are destroyed with the correct priority, method (refer to the explanations above) and correct timing, things should never get out of hand. As stated, the killing order of the Tentacles is as follows: Eye Tentacles first, Giant Eye Tentacles second and Giant Claw Tentacles last. Assignments should be made accordingly.

The only classes that should stop to damage the Flesh Tentacles in C’Thun’s stomach are the ranged DPS classes and rogues with Evasion available. Healers and melee should apply a DoT before immediately stepping on the pad behind the Tentacles to resurface above. Make sure to position yourself at the center to avoid getting bounced against the wall and killed.

It is imperative that the Flesh Tentacles are killed right after a Giant Claw Tentacle has died, a Giant Eye Tentacle is dead or near dead, and the smaller Eye Tentacles recently eliminated, in order to make sure that the DPS on C’Thun in his weakened state is optimized rather than dealing with these.

This is an optional encounter with very unique dynamics. It is composed of Lord Kri, Princess Yauj and Vem, which must be fought at the same time. The loot gained is determined by the order the Bug Family is killed. Each of the three share a loot table, but each has its own unique loot as well that will only come into play if they are defeat last. This allows the encounter to become expontentially more challenging if certain items are sought after. The easiest way is to kill Lord Kri first, then Princess Yauj and finally Vem. The hardest way is to kill Lord Kri last.

Berserker Charge: Charges at a random target, knocking it back and dealing normal damage plus 300

Knockdown: Deals minimal damage to an enemy and its nearby allies, stunning them for 2 seconds. Afflicts up to 5 targets.

Strategy

Different strategies must be employed according to the order of which the three bugs are being killed.


Raid placement on the Bug Family.

Vem Last
This is the easiest of all paths to take and recommended to those new to the battle. Position the raid according to the illustration and initiate the pull. Quickly dispatch of Lord Kri, making sure to stay away from his front and the poison cloud that spawns upon death. The boss will be tanked in such a way that the healers can avoid receiving the damage from Toxic Volley, whereas casters must adjust their placement according to the amount of poison stacks and Nature damage mitigation.

Princess Yauj Last

At this point, Yauj will consume Kri. Two or three tanks must be on Vem at all times to alternate between taunts, whereas the rest will focus on Yauj. It’s important to target Vem with ranged attacks while all rogues pile on Yauj, making sure that she is kicked at all times when her claws begin to glow golden in order to interrupt her heal. When Vem dies, Yauj rushes over to consume him and gains the Vengeance ability. II - Viscidus



The battle with Viscidus is a very unique encounter in the sense that it is almost completely based around ice attacks. This means that Mages play a significant role in the encounter, but all damage dealers will be useful after acquiring some items and consumables. This boss requires a high Nature Resistance value as it uses AoE poison bolts which have direct damage along with DoTs. He resets if taken outside of his room, meaning that it is possible to run out before an impending wipe.

Preparation

Adequate Nature Resistance equipment

Greater Nature Protection Potion x2 (One pre-popped, one if the first one has been depleted)

Elixir of Poison Resistance Used for the purpose of dispelling the Poison DoT during the encounter (does not share cooldown with other consumables and can currently be used indefinitely on Kronos without incurring the intended cooldown. It should therefore be brought in excessive amounts).

Coldrage Dagger with Lifestealing enchant x2 Used by all melees in order to freeze Viscidus through Frost-proc damage.

Ice damage-based wand To be used by all casters without Frost abilities in order to freeze Viscidus.

Frost Oil To be used on Coldrage Dagger in order to increase the rate of Frost damage application to Viscidus.

Goblin Sapper Charge To be used by the whole raid during the Shattering Phase in order to facilitate the kills on Globs of Viscidus.

Abilities and Attacks

Passive: Takes 50% reduced damage from all attacks.

Basic Melee: Melees for ~1000 damage on a NR-geared tank. Viscidus hits very fast at first, but progressively slows down.

Poison Shock: AoE Nature attack that deals ~1200 Nature damage. Has a radius of 15 yards - be careful to stay away from Viscidus when not trying to shatter him once frozen.

Poison Bolt Volley: AoE spell cast every 10 seconds that hits for resistible ~1500 Nature damage and applies a dispellable DoT that deals ~500 Nature damage every 2 seconds for 10 seconds, similar to Lord Kri and Princess Huhuran. The volley ignores LoS and has a range of 100 yards. Nature Resist gear can greatly mitigate the direct damage, but the DoT is unresistable and therefore highly lethal. Poison curing must be set up for the raid so that no one is hit by more than one tick of the DoT, including the use of Elixir of Poison Resistance.

Summon Toxic Slime: Toxin Clouds are spewed out every 30-40 seconds, dealing ~1500 unresistable damage and reduces movement speed by 40%. This ability is similar to Lord Kri’s Death Special. A single bolt will predate the Toxin Cloud, so it is possible to move away from the landing point. The clouds look fairly small, but the damaging area is far greater than the visual representation of the zone.

Strategy

Circle the raid around Viscidus and unleash as many ice based attacks as possible upon him. Melee will use Frost Oil and Frost-proc weapons (e.g. Coldrage Dagger) to compensate for their lack of spells while other casters can use ice damage based wands. Mages should use their Rank 1 Frost Bolt, Frost Nova and Cone of Cold to freeze Viscidus as quickly as possible due to the fact that the strength of the abilities is irrelevant.

Anyone that can cleanse poison should be doing it actively as much as possible to mitigate Poison Bolt Volley, including the usage of Elixir of Poison Resistance. The Toxic Slime should be avoided at all costs and can be countered by having the main tank dragging Viscidus around and keeping him mobile during the fight.

Slowing Phase
The goal is to freeze him solid, and the progress is indicated by his attack and movement speed slowing down along with various messages. These are linked to different phases, described below:

"Viscidus begins to slow." - Movement and attack speed reduced by 15%. This phase begins after 100 frost abilities were used against the boss.

"Viscidus is freezing up." - Movement and attack speed reduced by 30%. This phase begins after 150 frost abilities were used against the boss.

"Viscidus is frozen solid." - Movement and attacks cease completely. At this point the raid must switch to melee attacks to shatter him. This phase begins after 200 frost abilities were used against the boss.

Frozen Phase
When he is frozen solid, every damage dealer in the raid must close in upon him and use melee attacks and other abilities, e.g. Goblin Sapper Charge and Crystal Force, to prevent him from thawing out. When Viscidus has received enough damage, he shatters into an army of smaller Globs. Destroy as many as possible while they make their way to the center to reform Viscidus. They will move slowly at first, but continuously and steadily gathering speed over time.

Messages that indicate the progress will also appear similar to the Slowing Phase in the following manner:

"Viscidus begins to crack." - Continue melee attacks.

"Viscidus looks ready to shatter." - Continue melee attacks.

"Viscidus explodes." - Viscidus shatters into multiple small "Globs of Viscidus" in a circle around the room. Once they reach the center, he will be reformed.

Shattering Phase and Globs of Viscidus
Every time Viscidus is shattered, he splits into 1-20 Globs of Viscidus, each one representing 5% of his HP. Each Glob takes 10K-15K damage to kill and is immune to all slows and stuns. Destroy as many as possible while they make their way to the center to reform Viscidus, making sure to focus fire as dealing damage to one has no effect unless it is killed. They will move slowly at first, but continuously and steadily gathering speed over time. Eventually he becomes smaller and smaller while spawning less and less Globs, indicating the total progress of the encounter.

III - Ouro the Sandworm



Preparation

Greater Nature Protection Potion x2 (One pre-popped, one if the first one has been depleted)

Abilities and Attacks

Phase 1

Sand Blast: Spews sand at any target in a frontal hemisphere (180 degrees) around Ouro, dealing 3.5K-4.5K Nature damage and stunning them for 5 seconds. This is Ouro’s deadliest attack. It can be fully resisted or not - damage is not reduced/blocked since it has an incapacitating effect that makes it a binary spell. Ouro uses the ability in the direction of the person with the highest threat and being hit by Sand Blast clears all threat for that person. It is generally cast every 20-25 seconds and has 45 yards range, meaning it can easily be outranged. Healers should immediately begin to heal those hit.

Sweep: Ouro unleashes a melee attack, dealing 1K-2.5K damage and causing anyone in range to be knocked away. This often results in a temporary aggro switch to the ranged group, which could be lethal due to Sand Blast. If Ouro is not engaged by some sort of melee combat for five seconds, he burrows under the ground prematurely, causing a great amount of havoc. It is also crucial that the tank that was swept does not go into melee range due to the fact that Ouro will return to that warrior, and chances are that this player is not at full health.

Burrow: Every 1.5 minutes or 3 minutes, Ouro retreats underground for 30 seconds, becoming untargettable and undamagable. He will not submerge if he is busy casting Sand Blast or Sweep, but will do so in the case of no player being in melee range of the boss.

During this time the raid will be avoiding the Dirt Mound Quakes. Make sure the entire raid converges upon the center of the room ten seconds before he re-emerges, so that the point he chooses to burst forth from is predictable and not in the vicinity of a wall. If this were to happen, positioning can be tricky to maintain.

Dirt Mound Quake: When Ouro burrows, he sends out a series of quakes that deal ~1500 Nature damage. Spread out and head for the walls when this happens, but make sure to head back to the center of the room before he emerges again.

Ground Rupture: When Ouro emerges from a Burrow, anyone caught under him receives 2000 physical damage. This damage is rarely fatal, so normally it is preferable to soak it in order to maintain proper positioning of the boss.

Scarab Swarm: As Ouro emerges from his burrow, he spawns sixteen Scarabs, dealing 300 physical damage on cloth and 40-150 on tanks. Tauntable for a short time, but will pick an initial target and stick to them for the whole duration. Since they deal moderately low damage, they should be handled using crowd control while proper positioning is established. While it is possible to defeat these Scarabs and can be done so with relative ease, they despawn on their own after 45 seconds.

Phase 2

Berserk: At 20% health, Ouro gains 150% increased attack speed and 100% increased damage. If his primary target pulls back out of melee range, he’ll launch boulders at that player dealing ~6000 physical damage (similar to the Ragnaros encounter). Ouro does not burrow anymore at this point, but will unleash Dirt Mound Quakes, Sand Blasts and Scarabs the entire time. Keeping the main tank is paramount during this phase.

Strategy

This is an encounter that requires proper raid positioning along with reacting to each of his abilities. It is important that the off-tanks are standing 90 degrees from the main tank on both respective sides during the pull to avoid receiving damage from Sand Blast. The raid should be positioned using 3 groups spread with 120 degrees between them to prevent the number of deaths in case the positioning goes wrong.


Raid placement on Ouro.

The ranged DPS need to burn down the boss as much as possible during the intervals when he is up without the need to worry about pulling aggro. This is because Ouro will not move to engage someone at range, and is therefore unaffected by the threat table.
Melee DPS is more tricky in the sense that they want to avoid the Sweep ability, meaning that they only have a few moments to deal damage to Ouro. Even then, the boss’ aggro is unpredictable for anyone in melee range, meaning that he can easily turn to a player and obliterate them in one or two hits if off-tanks are unable to taunt him in time and move him to the main tank position.

When Ouro does burrow, staying alive takes precedence over everything else. It is important to mitigate as much damage as possible from the Dirt Mound Quakes by constantly moving around, and Greater Nature Protection Potions can be used if need be.

In order to determine where Ouro will surface, it is imperative that everyone in the raid stays in the center of the room as the submerging phase is about to end. Once he appears, multiple tanks will be on Ouro at all times to ensure that he does not submerge more than necessary. The Scarabs will predominantly be handled with crowd control abilities since they despawn after 45 seconds, e.g. fear, frost novas, frost traps.

During the second phase at 20% health, healers and ranged DPS should stay in position as best as possible while making sure not to stand still for an extended period to prevent receiving damage from Dirt Mound Quake. Melee DPS should now use all cooldowns available, e.g. Recklessness and Execute while the main tank uses Shield Wall. Ouro has roughly 400K damage in this phase, meaning that he can be taken down quite quickly with adequate DPS from the all of the cooldowns used.
